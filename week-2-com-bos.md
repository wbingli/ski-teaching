# Week 2: Center of Mass & Base of Support

**PSIA Fundamental 1**: Control the relationship of the center of mass to the base of support to direct pressure along the length of the skis.

**Goal**: Develop proper fore-aft balance to maintain uniform pressure along the entire ski.

---

## Why This Matters

When CoM is properly positioned over BoS:
- **Entire ski engages** with the snow (tip to tail)
- **Better turn initiation** - tips can enter the turn
- **More stability** - balanced athletic stance
- **Less fatigue** - efficient use of skeletal structure

### Common Problems from Poor Fore-Aft Balance
- **Too far back**: Tips light, hard to initiate turns, quad burn
- **Too far forward**: Tails wash out, loss of control

---

## The Body Movement

> Position center of mass over the center of the feet by varying the flex of ankle, knee, and hip joints throughout the turn.

**Key Cue**: "Shin pressure into the tongue of the boot"

## PSIA Concepts Explained

### Center of Mass (CoM)
- The point where your body's mass is concentrated
- Approximately at your belly button / pelvis
- Moves as you move

### Base of Support (BoS)
- The area between your skis and boots
- Wider stance = larger BoS = more stable
- Narrower stance = smaller BoS = more dynamic

### The Relationship
- CoM should remain within the BoS for balance
- As terrain changes, must adjust to keep CoM over BoS
- **Transition** (turn initiation): CoM moves toward new turn
- **Shaping** (middle of turn): CoM moves inside turn
- **Finish**: CoM centered, ready for next transition

### Ankle Flex is Key
- Ankles are the primary joint for fore-aft adjustment
- Stiff ankles = poor balance response
- Flexible ankles = quick adjustments possible

## Dry Land Workout

This week focuses on balance and ankle mobility - essential for CoM/BoS control!

**1. Single Leg Balance with Eyes Closed** (20 seconds each leg)
- Stand on one foot, close your eyes
- Notice how much harder it is without visual reference
- *Why it helps*: Develops proprioception - feeling where your body is in space

**2. Ankle Circles** (10 circles each direction, each foot)
- Lift one foot, rotate ankle slowly in circles
- Do both clockwise and counter-clockwise
- *Why it helps*: Ankle mobility is key to fore-aft balance adjustments

**3. Squats with Shin Lean** (10 reps x 3 sets)
- Regular squat, but focus on pressing shins forward
- Feel pressure on front of ankle (like pressing into ski boot tongue)
- *Why it helps*: Mimics the athletic ski stance with forward shin pressure
